Adjust

The adjustments that matter, without the suite bloat

Most photos don't need 200 tools — they need six good sliders. This editor covers the adjustments photographers actually reach for: exposure to rescue dark shots, contrast for punch, saturation for colour intensity, temperature to fix that blue indoor cast or add golden-hour warmth, vignette to pull the eye inward, and sharpening for a crisp finish. Every slider updates the preview live, and the export re-applies your edits at full resolution.

The histogram up top is the same instrument pro tools show: the distribution of tones from shadows on the left to highlights on the right. If the graph slams against the right edge, you've blown the highlights; against the left, you're crushing shadows. Drag exposure or contrast and watch it move — it's the fastest way to learn what these sliders really do.

One-tap looks

  • Vivid — saturated and contrasty, made for product shots and travel photos.
  • Mono — true black & white with lifted contrast.
  • Warm — golden cast and gentle saturation, flattering for portraits.
  • Film — softened contrast, warm shift and a subtle vignette.
  • Fade — the matte, low-contrast look that flatters moody scenes.

Presets just position the sliders — tap one, then keep adjusting. Nothing here is destructive until you download, and even then the original file on your device is untouched. Need crop or rotation too? They live in the crop and rotate tools, or all together in the full editor.

Common questions

Does the editor reduce quality?

Edits are computed once at export on the full-resolution image. Save as PNG for lossless output or JPG/WebP at 93% quality for smaller files.

Why do my edits look different at export?

They shouldn't — the preview applies identical math on a downscaled copy for speed, and the export re-runs it on every original pixel.

Is anything uploaded?

No. All processing is local Canvas work; the page works offline once loaded.
